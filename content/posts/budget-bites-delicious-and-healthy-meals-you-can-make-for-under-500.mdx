# Budget Bites: Delicious and Healthy Meals You Can Make for Under ₹500

Eating healthy and delicious meals doesn't have to break the bank. In today's world, where food prices are constantly rising, finding affordable yet nutritious options can feel like a challenge. But fear not! This blog post is your guide to creating satisfying and healthy meals for under ₹500. We'll explore some budget-friendly recipes and tips to keep your wallet happy and your body nourished.

## 1. The Power of Pulses: Lentil Soup

Lentils are a nutritional powerhouse and incredibly affordable. A simple lentil soup is packed with protein, fiber, and essential vitamins and minerals. For this recipe, you'll need:

*   1 cup red lentils (approx. ₹30)
*   1 onion, chopped (approx. ₹10)
*   2 carrots, chopped (approx. ₹15)
*   2 cloves garlic, minced (approx. ₹5)
*   4 cups vegetable broth (made from bouillon cubes, approx. ₹10)
*   1 tsp cumin powder (approx. ₹5)
*   Salt and pepper to taste
*   Optional: A squeeze of lemon juice (₹5)

**Instructions:** Sauté the onion, carrots, and garlic in a pot. Add the lentils, vegetable broth, cumin powder, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender. Add lemon juice for extra flavor. This hearty soup is a complete meal in itself and provides ample servings.

## 2. Egg-cellent Option: Egg Curry with Rice

Eggs are a versatile and inexpensive source of protein. Egg curry paired with rice is a fulfilling and budget-friendly meal. Here's what you need:

*   4 eggs (approx. ₹20)
*   1 onion, chopped (approx. ₹10)
*   1 tomato, chopped (approx. ₹10)
*   1 inch ginger, grated (approx. ₹5)
*   2 cloves garlic, minced (approx. ₹5)
*   1 tsp turmeric powder (approx. ₹5)
*   1 tsp chili powder (adjust to taste) (approx. ₹5)
*   1 tsp coriander powder (approx. ₹5)
*   Salt to taste
*   1 cup rice (approx. ₹30)

**Instructions:** Boil the eggs, peel, and set aside. Sauté the onion, ginger, and garlic in a pan. Add the tomatoes and cook until softened. Add the turmeric, chili, and coriander powders, and salt. Cook for a few minutes. Add a cup of water and bring to a simmer. Gently add the boiled eggs and simmer for another 5-7 minutes. Serve hot with cooked rice.

## 3. Veggie Delight: Vegetable Stir-Fry with Noodles

Stir-fries are a quick and easy way to use up leftover vegetables. Combine them with noodles for a satisfying and healthy meal. Consider these ingredients:

*   1 packet noodles (approx. ₹20)
*   1/2 cup chopped cabbage (approx. ₹10)
*   1/2 cup chopped carrots (approx. ₹10)
*   1/2 cup chopped bell peppers (any color) (approx. ₹20)
*   2 tbsp soy sauce (approx. ₹10)
*   1 tbsp vinegar (approx. ₹5)
*   1 tsp ginger-garlic paste (approx. ₹5)
*   Optional: A pinch of chili flakes (₹5)

**Instructions:** Cook the noodles according to package instructions. Stir-fry the vegetables in a pan with ginger-garlic paste. Add the soy sauce, vinegar, and chili flakes (if using). Add the cooked noodles and toss well. Serve hot.

## 4. South Indian Staple: Sambar and Rice

Sambar is a flavorful lentil-based vegetable stew that's a staple in South Indian cuisine. It's healthy, delicious, and budget-friendly. Key ingredients:

*   1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas) (approx. ₹25)
*   Mixed vegetables (drumstick, pumpkin, okra, eggplant) (approx. ₹50)
*   1 onion, chopped (approx. ₹10)
*   1 tomato, chopped (approx. ₹10)
*   Sambar powder (approx. ₹15)
*   Tamarind pulp (approx. ₹10)
*   Mustard seeds, curry leaves for tempering (approx. ₹5)
*   1 cup rice (approx. ₹30)

**Instructions:** Cook the toor dal until soft. Sauté the vegetables, onion, and tomato. Add the cooked dal, sambar powder, and tamarind pulp. Simmer until the vegetables are cooked. Temper with mustard seeds and curry leaves. Serve hot with rice.

## 5. Spiced Chickpea Curry (Chana Masala) with Roti

Chickpeas are an excellent source of protein and fiber, making chana masala a filling and healthy meal. Served with roti (Indian flatbread), it's a complete and satisfying option.

*   1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight) (approx. ₹30)
*   1 onion, chopped (approx. ₹10)
*   1 tomato, chopped (approx. ₹10)
*   Ginger-garlic paste (approx. ₹5)
*   Chana masala powder (approx. ₹15)
*   Turmeric powder (approx. ₹5)
*   Chili powder (approx. ₹5)
*   Salt to taste
*   Roti (approx. ₹5 per roti, estimate 4 rotis) (approx. ₹20)

**Instructions:** Boil the soaked chickpeas until tender. Sauté the onion, ginger-garlic paste, and tomatoes. Add the chana masala powder, turmeric powder, chili powder, and salt. Cook until the spices are fragrant. Add the boiled chickpeas and simmer for 15-20 minutes. Serve hot with roti.

## Conclusion

Eating healthy on a budget is entirely possible. By focusing on affordable staples like lentils, eggs, vegetables, and chickpeas, you can create delicious and nutritious meals for under ₹500. Get creative with your spices and ingredients, and don't be afraid to experiment! With a little planning and resourcefulness, you can enjoy tasty and healthy meals without emptying your wallet. Happy cooking!
